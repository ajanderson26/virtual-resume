Political Map of Africa

This map is a result of a political geography mapping challenge in my first cartography class. The goal of this assignment was to improve labeling skills by managing over 200 labels. In this lab, I wanted to utilize Eduard Imhof guidelines for labeling by using labels to invoke the feature. This is why many labels of coastal features and countries have a slight curve to them. I also utilized the halo technique again in this map to use contrast as a way to show which labels were more important than others (such as country labels) and establish proper visual hierarchy. Lastly, leader lines were used to emphasize locational importance as well as create space for labels that would be improperly placed on the scale of visual hierarchy if no leader lines were used otherwise. I am proud of the way that this labeling challenge was completed although not without certain adjustments to use of color.
